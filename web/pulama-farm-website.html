Our Farm

Pulama Farm is a quiet growing place above the Kona coast.

Set in Makalei Estates, Pulama Farm is described as a 3-acre estate where Māmaki is grown at about 1,350 feet elevation. The leaves are harvested in very small batches, dried in the Kona sun, and selected carefully to preserve both flavor and character.

Pulama Farm embraces a deep connection to the land, nurturing each Māmaki tea plant with careful cultivation and intentional harvesting. Rooted in a sustainability-first approach, the farm thrives without synthetic fertilizers or pesticides, ensuring every sip reflects the purity and care of the Hawaiian soil.

About Māmaki

The “healer of the forest” in a modern daily tea ritual.

Māmaki, or Pipturus albidus, is a plant long associated with traditional Hawaiian use. It is earthy, slightly nutty, caffeine-free, and perfect for mindful daytime drinking or calm evening routines.

Pipturus albidusA native Hawaiian herbal tea plant cherished for generations.

How to Brew

Simple brewing for a darker amber or maroon-brown cup.

Use about 1 tablespoon of crushed leaves, or around 10 to 15 leaves, per quart of boiling water and steep until the color deepens.

1

Measure

Use 1 tablespoon of crushed leaves, roughly 1 to 2 larger leaves or 10 to 15 leaves per quart.

2

Steep

Boil or steep for 10 to 20 minutes depending on the depth and strength you want.

3

Watch the color

Brew until the tea reaches a dark amber or maroon-brown tone.

4

Enjoy again

The leaves can be re-steeped 2 to 3 times for additional cups.

Serving ideas

Serve Māmaki as iced tea with lemon or pair it with lemongrass, mint, or lemon for a brighter flavor profile.
